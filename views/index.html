What must you do at a STOP sign?

When stopping at a stop sign or stop line you need to come to a complete stop at or before the stop line (or intersection if there is no stop line), look and then give way to vehicles and/or pedestrians. Once it is safe, proceed.

Answer: Stop and give way to all traffic on the crossroad

What is the rule regarding overtaking at a pedestrian crossing?

Never overtake within 30 metres of a pedestrian crossing. While driving on roads with more than one lane, lane changing may be required to overtake another vehicle, to avoid a parked vehicle or when the vehicle ahead slows to turn at an intersection.

Answer: You must not overtake a vehicle that has stopped at a pedestrian crossing

What does a broken white line down the centre of the road mean?

Broken white lines are used to mark the middle of a two lane highway to separate traffic on both directions. Drivers are supposed to keep left but can cross the broken line for overtaking if situations permit.


Answer: You may cross the broken white line when overtaking or turning right, if it is safe to do so
